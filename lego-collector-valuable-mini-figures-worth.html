I'm a Lego collector - check your toy boxes for five Minifigures worth up to $20,000

Posted by Martina Birk on Thursday, August 8, 2024

THE top five most valuable LEGO Minifigures are worth up to $20,000 each.

You'll want to check your home for these — especially if you're a Star Wars fan.

Mr. Luxury (@mrluxurybrand) is a TikTok content creator who makes videos about luxurious and expensive things.

In a recent video, he detailed the five most expensive Lego mini-figures that collectors go crazy over.

Coming in at number five is the Lego Minifigure Series 10 Mr. Gold, worth around $1,786.

Released in 2010, Mr. Gold Minifigures were distributed randomly into LEGO Minifigures Series 10 blind bags, so customers had no idea if they would get a Mr. Gold inside.

Only 5,000 were made, making them an essential part of many Lego enthusiast's toy collections.

Slightly more valuable are the exclusive Lego Iron Man and Captain America Minifigures.

These two were part of a promotional Marvel Super Heroes set that was given away at the New York Toy Fair in 2012.

On the resale market, the toys can fetch upward of $1,900 each.

The third most expensive Lego Minifigure is the Black Suit Superman, worth around $2,117.

This piece was made exclusively for the 2023 San Diego Comic-Con, and there are only five in existence.

The design is a tribute to Superman's black suit, which the character wears to restore his energy.

While Marvel and DC-inspired Minifigures are valuable, Star Wars Minifigures command the highest prices of all.

GENUINE GOLD

The second most expensive Lego is a 14k gold C-3PO, worth between $10,000 and $15,000.

Only five of these toys are in existence after they were made in 2007 for a LEGO Star Wars 30th-anniversary contest.

So not only are these figures sought-after because they are made of genuine 14k gold, but also because of their rarity.

It may not surprise a Star Wars fan to learn that the most valuable Minifigure of all is a homage to Boba Fett.

The fan-favorite bounty hunter was immortalized in 14k carat gold — and produced in only two copies.

This incredibly rare LEGO is estimated to be worth the most of any piece, selling for over $20,000 on the collector's market.

Some of these LEGOs were given away in sets, so if you held on to your old collection over the years, make sure to check if you are the lucky owner of any of these valuable pieces.
