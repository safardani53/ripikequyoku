Distraught family share heartbreaking statement after Courtney Cowan and friend Lee Pickthall die in

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, March 18, 2024

THESE are the first pictures of the young pair who lost their lives when their car crashed off a country road in Scotland.

Courtney Cowan, 19, and Lee Pickthall, 25, died at the scene of the collision on Thursday in Dumfries and Galloway.

According to Police Scotland, the Audi A6 the pair were travelling in left the A710 after driver Lee lost control of the powerful vehicle at approximately 11pm.

Emergency services rushed to the scene of the accident, but Lee and Courtney were pronounced dead a short time after being recovered.

The road was shut for several hours as officials launched an investigation into the crash, with officers from Police Scotland appealing for any witnesses to contact them.

Devastated relatives have said their lives will never be the same as they come to terms with the loss of the “popular” young woman and driver Lee.

In a statement issued by Police Scotland, Ms Cowan’s family said: "We are devastated by the loss of our beautiful Courtney.

“She was a popular, lively young woman with a bright future ahead of her.

"Everyone loved her and our lives will never be the same again as we try to come to terms with her loss.

"We are overwhelmed by the messages of concern and condolences we have received at this sad time and we would like to thank everyone who has been in touch."

The pair’s social media accounts have been flooded with tributes following the tragedy.

On Facebook, Rachel Donowho wrote: “The whole of Dumfries are lost for words.

“Times like this make you realise you need to appreciate everyone more.

“Such sad sad news, my thoughts & love go out to both of your friends & family.”

Laura Ashley Thorburn said: “Will never be the same again without you, it’s hard to believe that your not here right now as we all write our status.

“You should still be here doing everyone’s head in Lee Pickthall, making us all laugh.

“My thoughts go out to everyone but mostly your mum and sister.

“Rest in peace pal, party hard!”

Emma Bennett wrote: “RIP Courtney and Lee, thoughts and prayers are with the family.”

And Liam Cameron commented: “RIP Courtney and Lee, taken far too soon. Thoughts are with your family and friends.”
