Cops investigate claims a woman was gang raped by five men in a caravan at a 'Friday night nudist pa

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, October 2, 2024

POLICE are investigating reports a woman was gang raped by up to five men at a nudist resort.

The alleged attack happened in her caravan at the Eureka Sun Club in Fawkham, Kent, about 2.30am on Saturday.

Earlier in the evening she had been talking some people inside a car at the 23-acre club.

Up to five men are said to have followed her back to her caravan before she was subjected to a "sustained assault", police say.

The club, which charges punters up to Â£60, is open from 10am almost every day of the year, invites couples, single females and single males to "join in the fun" at weekly parties.

Members are subject to a "clothing is not compulsory and is totally optional" dress code and are also invited to join in on 'Naked Friday' and 'Naked Wednesday' events.

Detectives are particularly keen to speak to a man who is believed to have been socialising with the victim in the immediate hours before the alleged rape took place.

The suspects are reported to have also stolen two mobile phones belonging to the victim.

The private members' club has 23 acres of grounds and boasts an outdoor heated swimming pool, sauna and Jacuzzis.

A statement on the club website said: "Liberate yourself too, if you wish, as Eureka is a naturist venue so you will find people walking around naked enjoying the grounds, sunbathing naked in the fields or naked around the swimming pool or the spa area as well as clothed and partially clothed.

"So, you see, Eureka really is a place to visit or stay whatever your attire preference."

A spokesperson for Kent Police said: "Our detectives are investigating a report that a woman was raped near Longfield on Saturday 17 June, 2017.

"The incident is alleged to have happened at the Eureka Sun Club in Manor Lane, Fawkham.

"Detectives are continuing work to establish the circumstances of the incident, and no arrests have yet been made.

"This is being treated as an isolated incident and there are no other reported offences that are being linked to this investigation."
