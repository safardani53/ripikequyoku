I wasn't sure if my wedding guest dress was 'too white' - people say they'd have me escorted out if

Posted by Martina Birk on Thursday, March 21, 2024

A FASHION lover has sparked debate with her controversial choice of wedding guest attire.

She expressed concerns that the style may be "too white" and commenters agreed - saying that they would forced her to leave.

Nicole Mckenzie (@nicolejackmckenzie) regularly shares videos of herself dressed to the nines in classy and elegant gowns.

In a TikTok video, she tried on a dress for an upcoming nuptial event that accentuated her figure and highlighted her blonde hair.

She expressed concerns about whether or not the color was a good choice for the wedding.

When the video began, she held up a cream-colored long gown with blue floral details and wore a white bathrobe.

The subtitle at the top of the video read: "Is this too white for a wedding?"

Nicole added in the video caption that she thought "the flowers make it acceptable."

Then, she showcased the dress and modeled it for her audience.

She accessorized the look with a green sparkly shoulder bag and suede nude pumps.

There were conflicting opinions in the comment section of the video.

Some people found it acceptable, while others were against it.

"Literally put in my dress code no florals with a base of white or cream for this very reason," a viewer commented.

Another wrote: "You'd be escorted out of my wedding but yeah whatever you want girl."

"It’s so beautiful but not for a wedding. Lots of brides are choosing white/ivory dresses with colored florals now for them or bridesmaids so I’d avoid," another added.

"If you have to ask...then it’s white. That’s white with blue flowers," a TikTok user chimed into the debate.
