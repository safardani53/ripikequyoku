The Mitsubishi L200 is one of the most unashamedly brawny pick-ups on market

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Sunday, June 9, 2024

AVAILABLE with the words “Barbarian” and “Warrior” emblazoned on them, the Mitsubishi L200 has always been one of the most unashamedly brawny pick-up trucks on the market.

Where Mercedes-Benz, Nissan and VW are busy refining their rides to appeal to those company car users looking for a cheeky tax break (rather than actually transporting heavy loads off-road), Mitsubishi is busy feeding its mud-plugging monster a diet of protein shakes and steaks.

The latest L200 has received some extra strengthening to key load-bearing areas of the chassis. This means it can now tow 3.5-tonnes when beefy three-axle braked trailers are attached to the revised tow bar, while a towing capacity of up to 3.1 tonnes is possible with one or two-axle braked trailers.

That might not mean much to your average punter, but if you own a boat or like to ride several different nags at the weekend, it will be music to the ears.

Of course, the L200 isn’t the first to manage this kind of towing trickery – the Ford Ranger, Isuzu D-Max and Nissan Navara are just as capable.

But the Mitsubishi trumps them as the only pick-up that can tow 3.5-tonnes on tarmac in four-wheel drive.

Hay there, big boy

On top of this, all new L200s come with the Trailer Stability Assist system as standard, which brakes independent wheels to bring things back in line should the trailer start to slip and slide.

But that’s not all, because the new L200 offers a bed payload of up to 590kg at its maximum towing weight – significantly greater than many rivals.

Its 2.4-litre diesel engine is strong and reasonably quiet, but the L200 isn’t the most comfortable pick-up on the market, due to the agricultural suspension that allows for such a huge payload.

I was impressed with the new ­Mitsubishi touchscreen system, though, which seamlessly links to smartphones via Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, meaning I didn’t have to rely on the manufacturer’s legendarily clunky sat-nav system.

With prices starting at £19,855, the new L200 even undercuts the refreshed Ssangyong Musso, which used to be the bargain of the bunch.

In this day and age, it might not be deemed cool to have the word “Barbarian” sprayed along your pick-up’s flanks, but the new L200 does what it says on the tin and absolutely steam-rollers those tough jobs.

Scoot away

FOR two weeks I commuted on an electric scooter – and loved every mad mile of it.

The Light 2 is a mid-range Inokim, with a top speed of 21mph and a range, we’re told, of 24 miles. I got more like 15. It weighs in at a sturdy 13.7kg and folds up for easy carrying.

Despite fantastic dual drum brakes and hefty tyres, riding it felt like a bit of a high-wire act.

But on my 50-minute commute, I never came off or felt out of control and the tyres gripped even the wettest asphalt.

If you want a fast, fun scoot to work, this could be it. From £1,130 at inokimuk.com.
