What does a shaman do?

Shamans perform a variety of practices depending upon their respective cultures.

These include: healing, leading a sacrifice, preserving traditions by storytelling and songs, fortune-telling, and acting as a psychopomp.

However, a single shaman may fulfil several of these functions.

Shamans are often thought to have the ability to heal the sick and to communicate with the other world.

They also escort the souls of the dead to the otherworld.

The term shamanism comes from the Manchu-Tungus word šaman.
