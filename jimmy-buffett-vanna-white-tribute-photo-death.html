Jimmy Buffett fans in tears as Vanna White pays tribute to late singer using sweet Wheel of Fortune

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, June 17, 2024

VANNA White has shared a winning photo with the late Jimmy Buffett and sweetly captioned it in Wheel of Fortune style.

The Margaritaville singer, who tragically died at 76 on Friday, was a longtime friend of the game show.

Vanna, 66, shared the snap with Jimmy on Instagram on Friday, just hours after the tragic news.

The iconic singer donned a salmon polo shirt and a huge smile, while Vanna wore a tropical dress, looking gorgeous as ever.

She captioned it: "M_RG_RIT_VILLE won’t be the same without you! We all love you Jimmy."

Vanna left off the A vowels as a nod to Jimmy's close ties to Wheel of Fortune.

'ONE OF A KIND!'

Fans were moved as one person commented: "A caption that only you could write, Vanna."

"Margaritaville won’t be the same without Jimmy," wrote another.

"It’s so sad!! One of a kind!! We love his music and his restaurants. Classics," wrote a third.

"I thought of you all when I heard the passing of Jimmy Buffett, so incredibly sad and my condolences to you as well as the family," wrote a fourth.

JIMMY SPINS BY WHEEL OF FORTUNE

The hitmaker made an unannounced appearance on a 2019 segment of the beloved show hosted by Vanna and Pat Sajak, 76.

The segment featured both hosts and was filmed inside Daytona Beach's new Latitude Bar & Chill restaurant.

Wheel of Fortune broadcasted a week-long series of Margaritaville-themed shows earlier that year, according to JournalOnline.

The viewer contest saw a grand prize winner given the choice of a new home in one of Buffet's private communities in either Daytona Beach, Florida, or Hilton Head, South Carolina.

The segment announced who won the prize and was aired on the show.

Wheel also remembered Jimmy with a snap from the same 2019 shoot with Pat in the frame as well.

It was captioned: "Wheel of Fortune was incredibly saddened to hear about the loss of our dear friend, Jimmy Buffett.

"Over the last 5 seasons the Margaritaville family has become a part of the Wheel family. You’ll always be in our hearts!"

The upcoming 41st season of Wheel premieres September 11 and is historic, as it marks Pat's last as host before he retires and Ryan Seacrest takes over in June 2024.

Vanna - at the puzzle board since 1982- is still negotiating her contract and demanding a hefty pay raise for the first time in 18 years amid his retirement.

Vanna has had a hard month as she also lost a friend in The Price Is Right's Bob Barker- whom she credited with giving her her big break.

'PASSED AWAY PEACEFULLY'

News of the music icon's death was announced by his family on social media in an emotional statement.

“Jimmy passed away peacefully on the night of September 1st surrounded by his family, friends, music and dogs. 

“He lived his life like a song till the very last breath and will be missed beyond measure by so many," the Margaritaville singer's family wrote in a statement.

He leaves behind his wife, Jane Slagsvol, whom he's been married to since 1977, and three children: Savannah, Sarah, and Cameron.

HOSPITALIZATION

Jimmy had been in and out of the hospital in the months preceding his death.

He was initially admitted to a hospital back in May, as reported by CNN.

The trip to the hospital forced the postponement of a Jimmy Buffett & the Coral Reefer Band concert scheduled to be held in Charleston, South Carolina at the time.

Jimmy shared the news on his personal website and on social media at the time.

He wrote: "Two days ago, I was just back from a trip to the Bahamas, thawing out from the California 'winter tour,' and chomping at the bit to get to Charleston.

"I had to stop in Boston for a check-up but wound up back in the hospital to address some issues that needed immediate attention."

Jimmy did not reveal what those issues were, nor did he share any details about the status of his health.

He concluded: "Growing old is not for sissies, I promise you.

"Thank you for your prayers and thoughts, your amazing years of loyalty, and just remember, 'NOT YET!'"

Months later, in July, he surprised an audience in Portsmouth, Rhode Island with a performance at Sunset Cove, a waterfront bar.

LASTING LEGACY

Jimmy released more than 50 albums and wrote countless iconic songs.

He is known for his hit singles Margaritaville, Come Monday, and Cheeseburger in Paradise.

A majority of his albums have gone gold, platinum, or multi-platinum.

Jimmy has performed on stages worldwide and has sold out numerous concert venues.

He won Country Music Association awards during his career and was inducted into the Nashville Songwriters Hall of Fame.
