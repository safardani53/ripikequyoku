What happened to the Norris Nuts dog?

Posted by Martina Birk on Thursday, August 15, 2024

FORMER Olympian Justin Norris and his wife Brooke are the head honchos of the Norris Nuts YouTube channel.

The duo posts family-orientated content on their channel alongside their six children, Sabre, Sockie, Biggy, Naz, Charm and Disco.

Who are Justin and Brooke Norris?

Justin is a former Australian professional swimmer best known for his butterfly and individual medley swimming.

The 41-year-old won the bronze medal in the 200 meters butterfly at the 2000 Summer Olympics in Sydney, Australia.

He competed against Michael Phelps at the Sydney Olympic Games.

Brooke, 41, is a Youtube content creator and the mother of all of the Norris Nuts.

What happened to the Norris Nuts dog?

Justin and Brooke updated their five million-plus YouTube subscribers on the death of their five-day-old puppy, Bubba.

The couple explains how their newly acquired pup was involved in a freak accident on the fifth night.

However, neither Justin nor Brooke went into detail on what caused Bubba's death - only explaining how their children witnessed the pup die.

"Justin was in the wrong place at the wrong time... The puppy was, I guess," Brooke says in the nine-minute YouTube video.

"Yeah, I didn't know what was happening anyway. What happened was the puppy got hurt, and then since the puppy was so little, the puppy died.

"Yeah, like it was super traumatic for everyone," Justin adds, to which Brooke cuts in and says, "everyone was there like we saw the whole thing."

"Basically, the kids just saw the puppy die. They were holding the puppy. So, it sort of sucked for them, for everyone," Justin said.

Brooke added: "I feel really bad for Justin because all he tried to do was something nice for everyone, and then in doing this nice act somehow in an evil twist of fate, Bubba died."

Brooke explains how neglect or negligence did not play a part in the pup's death.

The couple posted a video on July 24 titled, "NORRIS NUTS MEET THEIR NEW PUPPY Emotional," which showed them bringing home Bubba to their children.
