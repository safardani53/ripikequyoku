Gatorade confirms it has discontinued entire lineup of flavors - fans say they were the 'best' in th

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, May 24, 2024

FANS are upset to learn that their favorite lineup of products has been discontinued by Gatorade.

Over the years, the sports drink company, owned by giant PepsiCo, has introduced different series' of beverages to appeal to different types of customers.

This includes healthier products that do not contain artificial colors, which studies have linked as a cause of hyperactivity.

That's part of the reason why PepsiCo came up with Gatorade Organic.

However, fans of the series met with some bad news after asking where the product had been on X.

The sports drink company said in response that "Gatorade Organic has been discontinued, but we'll let our team know you're looking for it!

READ MORE ON GATORADE

Previously, the Gatorade Organic series featured multiple flavors including strawberry, passion fruit, and mixed berry.

Some of the ingredients included citric acid, potassium chloride, sodium citrate, and sea salt.

While there were no artificial flavors, a 16.9 fluid-ounce bottle had a total of 29 grams of sugar.

Walmart previously sold them in three packs for $18.69, which equals 9.2 cents per ounce.

And fans of Gatorade Organic are saddened that it's gone.

"Fantastic Taste. Love having this option for my family. Extremely disapointed that this hasn't been restocked," one Walmart five-star review read.

Another fan said: "Best of the Garorate!! Excellent."

And a third wrote: "Love the organic Gatorade.No colors( its clear) or added flavors etc. Taste light, with a nice hint of fruit flavors .I usually don't like flavored drinks but i need Gatorade when I'm out in the sun for long periods and this one is perfect."

Overall, Walmart customers rated the Gatorade Organic products 4.2 out of five stars.

That's Gatorade Fit, which was launched by the company last year.

Instead of artificial dies, Gatorade uses watermelon juice concentrate to deliver the appearance.

Also, unlike Gatorade Organic, it includes no added sugar.

It is sweetened with stevia, a zero-calorie sweetener that's extracted from its namesake plant.

Gatorade Fit can be bought in the following flavors: Citrus Berry, Tropical Mango, Watermelon Strawberry, and Blackberry Raspberry.

They all sell in 16.9-ounce bottles for $1.38 each.

MORE WHERE THAT CAME FROM

But this isn't the only item that Gatorade has discontinued recently.

Another that has been axed is the G2 Blueberry Pomegranate flavor.

The label described it as a "low-calorie thirst quencher" option, which is what all of its G2 varieties aim to bring to the table.

Its ingredients consisted of artificial flavoring and coloring, as well as sugar and sucralose.

Also, Gatorade has discontinued another favorite known as the Gatorade Flow Kiwi Strawberry.

The drink featured a greenish look with its yellow 5 and blue 1 artificial coloring.

The product details on Walmart described it as having a "smooth finish and bold flavor" with "proven hydration" and electrolytes.
