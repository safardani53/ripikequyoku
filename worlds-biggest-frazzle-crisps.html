Shopper stunned at finding worlds biggest Frazzle in bag of crisps The Sun

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, March 17, 2024

A GIANT Frazzle is going viral after a surprised shopper shared a photograph of his supersized snack on Twitter.

Sean Stewart, 22, was stunned when he opened the bag and pulled out a crisp at least three times the length of a normal Frazzle.

Sean, of Cumbernauld, north Lanarkshire, Scotland, bought the packet of Frazzles as part of a £1 multipack from an Iceland in Glasgow’s West End on Monday.

He took to Twitter to share the image of his with the caption: "Check the size a this frazzle man. Am frazzled looking at it".

The bacon bonanza image has clocked up more than 5,000 likes online with one fan claiming it's the "world's biggest".

The giant snack has caused disbelief among social media users.

Some people are questioning just how the crisp even managed to fit in the bag.

SUPER FRAZZLE

Rhodri Wyn Jones said: "I can’t understand how this is possible.

“Unless you’ve grafted one frazzle to another to make one big super frazzle?!"

Sean responded: "The bag was already open and it was poking out. Was raging. Thought it would be soggy when a pulled this out - a Mary Poppins bag a Frazzles."

Another person commented: "That's got to be a Guinness world record of a Frazzle.

A supersized crunchy nut cornflake hit the headlines last year when a student revealed a picture of a breakfast flake measuring six inches long.

While Frazzles made headlines back in March after the crisps failed to make the top twenty in Channel 5’s controversial ranking of Britain’s best crisps.

MOST READ IN NEWS

Sky Sports presenter Hayley McQueen and Twitter account The Crisp Connoisseur were among the many to criticise the omission.

Frazzles were first produced in the UK in 1975.

Despite being bacon-flavour, the corn-based crisps are suitable for vegetarians.
