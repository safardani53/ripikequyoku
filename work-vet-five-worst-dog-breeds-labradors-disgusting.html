I work at a vet - the top five worst dog breeds, Labradors are disgusting and another is aggressive

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, February 23, 2024

A VETERINARY staff member has shared her top five worst dog breeds, based on her professional experience.

She gained perspective and opinions from her colleagues with someone saying that Labradors are disgusting, while another pet breed was aggressive.

Staff member Angel (@angelemurray02) works in a veterinarian's office.

In a TikTok video, she gave viewers a sneak peek into her work day and how she interacts with her coworkers.

Throughout the clip, she panned the camera around the room and let them share their personal thoughts.

The subtitle at the start read: "Top five worst dog breeds according to the veterinary staff."

Angel went around the room and allowed several people to list their choices.

A blonde woman in burgundy scrubs answered first and named a weiner dog, Chihuahua, English Bulldog, Basset, and Shar Pei.

Next, a redheaded woman said a Chihuahua, Husky, German Shepherd, Commodore, and "any little dog."

The next coworker listed: a German Shepherd, an Aussie, a Rottweiler, and a Chihuahua.

Halfway through the video, Angel chimed in and offered her own options, such as a Kai Ken, Great Dane, and Chihuahua.

As she spoke, a coworker could be heard in the background loudly saying Labrador Retriever.

Then, Angel continued speaking and added a "Lab" to her list, while the same coworker called the animal disgusting.

"I mean, I would own a Lab, but I don't like them," she said. "I need one more...an English Bulldog or a Chow."

Following the content creator's sentiments, another coworker voiced that "a Chihuahua for sure," and a German Shepherd, while she also wasn't a fan of Labs either.

Her last choice was an English Bulldog because "they got too many problems."

The video continue with a few more coworkers who listed their top five worst dog breeds.

Several pet owners offered their two cents in the comment section of the video.

"German shepherds are my absolute #1, 99 percent of them that come in are either aggressive, dangerously submissive, or dangerously nervous," one commented.
