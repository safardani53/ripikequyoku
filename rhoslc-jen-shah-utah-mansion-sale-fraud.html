RHOSLCs Jen Shah forced to move on as $4M Utah rental home goes up for sale before stars block

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Saturday, August 3, 2024

REAL Housewives of Salt Lake City's Jen Shah is being forced to give up her life of luxury as her $4million rental mansion goes on the market, The Sun can exclusively reveal.

The disgraced reality star, who was arrested on federal fraud charges back in April for her alleged involvement in a huge telemarketing scheme, could be facing up to 30 years behind bars if convicted.

Photographs show the jaw-dropping timber frame property, nicknamed the "Shah Ski Chalet," which boasts three master suites, two additional bedrooms and eight bathrooms.

Fans of the Bravo show were led to believe Jen and husband Sharrieff owned the property, before it was revealed the couple were only renting.

The home has actually been owned by Texas-based real estate rental company, Univesco Inc., since 2014, and The Sun can report it went on the market on May 25.

Property records show the seller has already accepted an offer, and the home is now "pending or under contract."

An agent for Coldwell Banker confirmed the home sold before it went on the market.

An online description reads: "At the end of a quiet cul-de-sac perched upon a bluff above the 8th and 10th holes of the renowned Tom Fazio designed Golf Club and Spa, this residence boasts panoramic views and serenity."

Jen bragged during one episode of RHOSLC that she loved "throwing big, over-the-top extravagant parties" at the multi-story home, which has its own bar and stone fireplaces.

Jen and her husband were also listed at a separate rental in Utah, although it's not known where they are currently living.

Snaps show their former home offers plush cream and gray furnishings and wall art, along with an indoor and outdoor jacuzzi set-up overlooking stunning views of the nearby golf course.

The luxurious nine-foot ceilings and ski-lodge-style design are also bonuses for any potential future renter.

The 9,420-square-foot home also has a pool table and wine cellar, perfect for entertaining guests.

The Sun could not substantiate Jen's claims they own multiple homes in Utah or New York, while her lawyers also said in court they did not believe she owned property.

Jen and her assistant, Stuart Smith, were arrested in Salt Lake City and charged with one count of conspiracy to commit wire fraud in connection with telemarketing and one count of conspiracy to commit money laundering.

She is reportedly still filming Real Housewives after being released from prison on a $1million bond and pleading not guilty.

An insider previously told Page Six: "Bravo knows full well that viewers are following every detail of this case and will continue to do so as it progresses, so producers plan to follow it just as closely and use whatever footage they legally can.
