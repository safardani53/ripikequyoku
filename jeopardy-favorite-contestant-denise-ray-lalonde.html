Jeopardy! stars vow to 'riot' if one fan-favorite player isn't invited back for next year's Second C

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Friday, July 26, 2024

A JEOPARDY! fan-favorite has gotten serious buzz after nearly beating super-champion Ray LaLonde last week.

Denise Carlon - who lost to the former 13-day winner by a mere $100 - left two major peers saying they'd "riot at dawn" if she isn't invited back on.

Denise Carlon of Seaside Heights, New Jersey, was dominating during what was Ray Lalonde's eventual last win, quickly passing the $10,000 mark and leading.

She did a great Dr. Evil impression to correctly get a clue which was posed on Jeopardy!'s Instagram, and almost beat big winner Ray who lost one night later with $386,000 total.

In the fall, Jeopardy! unveiled its first-ever Second Chance Competition where 18 non-winners got to return.

The two champs of that event then went directly to the 2022 Tournament of Champions - and both have backed Denise for the next one.

'BRING BACK DENISE!'

The terrific Rowan Ward who went viral as a result of being in the competition tweeted after Denise's episode: "If Denise doesn't get a call for #jeopardysecondchance, I riot at dawn.

"Seriously, though. She was not only an excellent player but so much fun to watch. I want to see her on Jeopardy! again!."

Jessica Stephens then also quote-tweeted Rowan and wrote: "Late to this party, but hooooooooooooooooooooooly s**t, Denise.

"Played a great game while being classy as f**k to her competitors. This is now a Denise Carlon stan account."

Having the two champs of that past special event stamp their approval is rare so it's surely good sign fans may see Denise again.

BEST GAME IN MONTHS?

After an incredible comeback, Ray went into Final Jeopardy! in first place, with a total of $16,100.

Denise was right on his heels with $16,000 entering Final Jeopardy.

"A close, close match heading into Final, with only $100 separating the leaders," host Ken Jennings remarked.

The category was Historic Crimes, and the clue was: "Saying it was stolen by Napoleon, Self-styled Italian patriot Vincenzo Peruggia took it in 1911."

Denise responded: "What is the Mona Lisa?"

That was correct and she put the champ on the ropes when it was revealed she wagered everything - doubling her total to $32,000.

Finally, it was Ray's turn and he also correctly guessed, "What is the Mona Lisa" and he too wagered $16,000, leaving him with $32,100.

Ken announced: "By $100 sir, you are a lucky 13-day champion" which wound up being his last win.

On Jeopardy!'s Instagram after the episode, fans also backed Denise, since she had a winning personality and put up the biggest score against Ray in 13 games.

"She was a great contestant!! Smart and funny. She should definitely be invited back for the second chance tournament. She played a great game!!" one wrote.
