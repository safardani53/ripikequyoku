London commuters face travel hell as Waterloo AND Liverpool Street delays spark chaos

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, February 14, 2024

LONDONERS have faced an evening of travel hell as services across three major operators were hit with severe delays and cancellations as rush hour took hold.

Services were heavily affected between London Liverpool Street and Stratford, as well as London Waterloo and Clapham Junction, leaving thousands of commuters stranded.

A customer incident saw the line closed between Clapham Junction and Waterloo as emergency services were called at 4.20pm to deal with a person on the track at Queenstown Road.

Emergency services were called to the area after receiving reports of a person hit by a train just as rush hour began.

A British Transport Police spokesman confirmed that while a person was on the tracks, they had not been struck.

Power was shut off for 45 minutes as emergency services made their way to the person, and the line reopened at 5.20pm with severe delays and a string of cancellations.

South Western Railway urged customers to check their route before travelling and confirmed that tickets were being accepted on London buses and the Underground.

SWR trains are currently running with a delay of up to 40 minutes, with customers adviced to check for service alterations and cancellations this evening.

At present, all SWR services running to the West of England heading towards London Waterloo will terminate at Basingstoke and all West of England services heading towards Salisbury/Exeter St Davids will start from Basingstoke.

C2C are also accepting tickets on the London Fenchurch Street and Southend Central routes.

Elsewhere, a line-side fire saw services heavily disrupted between London Liverpool Street and Stratford, causing a backlog of over an hour.

Dozens of Greater Anglia and TfL Rail services were cancelled as a result, with timetables for both operators hit with multiple delays and revisions.

The disruption is expected to continue long into the evening, as hundreds of commuters remain stranded at Stratford and Liverpool Street stations.

Plumes of thick black smoke could be seen billowing from close to Stratford earlier this afternoon as firefighters attended the scene.

Chaotic scenes were also seen at Bethnal Green during rush hour after a fire alert saw services at the station suspended for a short time.

Severe delays were reported in the area on TfL rail, as dozens of services were cancelled.
