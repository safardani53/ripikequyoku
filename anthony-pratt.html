Anthony Pratt Career

Anthony Pratt started his first job at McKinsey & Co. It is a management consulting firm. Afterward, in the year 1982, he joined Visy as General Manager of its board.

Over there he served many years. In 1988, Anthony became Deputy Chairman of Visy Industries. As a chairman, he continued to serve for more than three years.

Further, he moved to the United States for managing the US branch of the company. Over there he worked so hard for his company and served for 15 years.

Anthony’s company grew by 15-fold in sales and earnings grew up to US$100 million in 1991. His company reached the 5th position from 46th position in largest corrugated box producer.

Anthony was honored by New York City Mayor Michael Bloomberg introduced Pratt with a declaration of 17 September 2013 as the Pratt Industries Day.

In the year 2016, Anthony was awarded the RISI North American Packaging CEO of the Year Award.

In the same year 2016, Anthony opened and worked on another 100% recycled paper mill. It was such a successful step made by him that his income immediately increased by US$1 Billion.

On the 4th of May 2017, In the presence of President Donald Trump, Anthony promised to invest $2 billion to create 5,000 high-paying manufacturing jobs.

In August 2017, Anthony made his second commitment in the presence of Australian Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbul that he would invest A$2 billion in Visy Australia to create 5,000 Australian manufacturing jobs.

In the year 2020, Anthony was honored by the “Executive Papermaker of the Year” by the leading industry publication PaperAge.

Again, he was honored by the name “North American CEO of the Year” by Fastmarkets RISI.

Following in the next year 2021, Anthropology promised to invest $2 billion to increase the recycled capacity of Australian glass bottles from 30% to 70%.
