Amy Winehouse's family 'betrayed' by her best friend Tyler James' tell-all book

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Tuesday, September 10, 2024

SHE was the finest talent of a generation – and as the tenth anniversary of Amy Winehouse’s death approaches, her ­legacy remains just as impressive.

But for the tragic star’s ­family, the milestone and plans to celebrate with a documentary telling her incredible story have been tarnished, after her best friend released a tell-all book about their time together.

Sharing his account of a close friend’s global success is all well and good, but I’m told Tyler James’s version of events clashes significantly with the recollections of her mum and dad, Janis and Mitch, who insist much of his memoir is off the mark.

The book release has left the family feeling “betrayed” by Tyler, who first met Amy when they were at theatre school aged 12. They thought he was the one person they thought would never cash in on her fame.

The book, My Amy: The Life We Shared, was published last week, but a family source tells me: “Tyler had been close since his early teens and they considered him one of their own. But they weren’t told or ­consulted about this book.”

The insider added: “They are particularly upset that Tyler has been claiming to be the only friend left who was not paid to be around Amy towards the end of her life, but many of her closest pals including her fiancé Reg Traviss are totally missing from his story.

“After all the financial support that Amy and her family gave Tyler, to hear these claims from him has left a bad taste in the mouth.” The family are outraged at what they say is a false picture of Amy, who died aged 27 on July 23, 2011.

They do not recognise the singer that James portrays in the book — details of which have infuriated Amy’s family, including his suggestion he was the only person close to her trying to help her beat her demons.

The source added: “Key ­people in her life have just been left out. Tyler paints ­himself as the only one trying to help Amy while others exploited her.

"He says Amy was on anti-depressants from 14 years old — it’s just not true — and there’s even a part where he says Mitch was upset that she had duetted with Tony Bennett.

“In fact, he was thrilled. Tony was a hero for them both and Amy said she chose the song because it was her father’s favourite. Tony and his family remained friends with the Winehouses afterwards. The tenth anniversary is going to be a difficult enough time, and this just makes it worse.”

Amy’s family point to other claims in the book they say are plain wrong — even including details of her funeral.

A spokesman for the Wine-houses confirmed: “Amy’s parents are disappointed that Tyler has gone ahead with this book without consulting them, and dispute many of his claims.”

As the tenth anniversary approaches, it feels the perfect time to celebrate Amy’s short life. Here’s hoping her family do not have to suffer yet more heartache, from people using the milestone as a chance to cash in.

RITA’S OUT ORA NIGHT LONG

RITA Ora was up to her neck in celebrities at her latest party – just like the dress she wore for it.

The Hot Right Now singer wore the see-through outfit for her pal Vas J Morgan’s Pride celebration in Los Angeles.

Also in attendance were Demi Lovato, Ashley Benson, Paris Jackson, Adam Lambert and Jess Glynne.

Rita and Vas posted videos of the bash on Instagram as they drank LGBT-themed ­cocktails and enjoyed an extravagant dinner.

The night before she had been at another event, hosted by ­rapper Common.

It’s amazing she has time for ­partying as she is currently working on new music with Charli XCX and Sigala.

ROMEO’S GOTA LICENCE TO CHILL

MOST kids who turn 17 and get their ­driving licence count themselves lucky if they get any old car.

But for Romeo Beckham not only is his first motor a pricey number but his insurance is eye-wateringly high.

The son of David and Victoria took his Maserati Levante, with prices starting at £58,000, out for a spin in London with pop star pal HRVY.

To insure the vehicle – a standard Levante has a 3.0-litre V6 engine with 345 brake horsepower – would set him back around £25,000, just a few grand shy of the national average wage of £31,461.

And that’s after he has managed the almost impossible tasking of finding a company bold enough to give him a quote.

It’s hardly Simon’s yellow Fiat Cinquecento on The Inbetweeners.

An insider said: “We all know who bought that car. The bank of mummy and daddy was very generous with that deposit. It’s quite mad as Romeo is 18 and only recently passed his driving test.”

Despite the stress of driving through the capital’s busy streets, the youngster looked relaxed in cargo trousers, a black T-shirt and baseball cap that covered up his bleach-blond hair. He completed the pose with a silver chain necklace and earrings.

Meanwhile former England captain David was spotted by a group of Scottish fans in Mayfair, West London.

One supporter, who nabbed him for a picture, told him to stick a £10 bet on Lyndon Dykes netting against England last night. The fan said: “Told David to put a tenner on a Lyndon hat-trick.”

James’ weigh forward

JAMES Corden has revealed he is not half the man he used to be since dieting, telling US shock jock Howard Stern: “So far I have lost about 35lb or a bit more.

“I feel like I’m getting better but there’s still a way to go. I’m trying to work out but I suck at it.

“It’s terrible. There are so few hours in the day that are just my own.”

The Late Late Show host previously told how he had taken up running, lifting weights and eating healthy chickpea pizzas.

He added: “I’ve got three young kids. We have the show and all these things. I feel like, ‘Hang on. I’ve got an hour when I’m on my own, and I’m doing something I absolutely hate. I should just be lying down’.

“I can do nothing like lying on a couch and staring at the corner of a rug.”

It’s Baby to Booby

WHEN the Spice Girls reunite again, Emma Bunton will have a new nickname to replace Baby Spice – Booby Spice, after her chest swelled when she was pregnant with son Beau in 2007.

Emma recalled thinking of the name with partner Jade Jones as she breastfed their new baby: “We came up with it in the hospital and my boobs had ballooned to epic proportions with the milk coming in.

“From that moment on, I was Booby Spice.”

She told the Sweat, Snot And Tears podcast: “It is odd when you think about it – the way other people react to women feeding their babies.

"There’s nothing more natural than a woman feeding her baby.

“Let’s face it, it’s not like a pair of boobs is anything new these days. Boobs are everywhere.”
