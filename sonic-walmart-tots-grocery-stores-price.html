Sonic set to bring fan-favorite crispy item to grocers including Walmart but customers are divid

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Tuesday, March 5, 2024

SONIC fans will be able to buy a popular menu item at grocers and store it at home – but not everyone loves the idea.

In fact, the fast-food chain has already launched its fan-favorite tots at Walmart.

They will be available in more than 4,000 of the big box retailer's SuperCenters and will launch in more grocery stores "later this year," according to a statement posted by Sonic.

The tots can be found in the frozen section and the ingredients consist of Idaho potatoes, seed oils, natural flavors, garlic powder, and onion powder.

To prepare them, you'll want to preheat your oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit, according to the baking instructions on the label.

A full bag takes up to 26 minutes to cook, while half of it takes between 18 and 20 minutes.

For air fryers, you'll want to preheat them to 360 degrees Fahrenheit and then cook them for between nine and 10 minutes with half the bag.

At Walmart, while prices can vary by location, a 28-ounce frozen bag can be purchased for $4.98.

DIVIDED REACTIONS

However, some customers have criticized the price point of the tots.

Shoppers responded to a post made by foodie @markie_devo on Instagram.

"They're just regular tots you’re only paying for the name. They should’ve did the onion rings," one user said.

Another wrote: "Tots are tots. The Kroger brand is just as good if you fry them."

But some customers were excited to make the Sonic tots a new addition to their freezer but are still calling on the fast food chain to bring its onion rings to grocery stores.

One fan said: "Def buying these.. if they did the onion rings tho [too]."

But rightfully so, customers might have a point about the Sonic brand being a tad bit expensive.

Walmart customers can buy its Great Value brand of tater tots for only $1.66 per pound, while Sonic's costs $2.85 per pound.

The U.S. Sun has reached out to Walmart for comment.

MORE BRANDS LAUNCHING NEW PRODUCTS

The move by Sonic comes as other major brands have been launching products for the summer.

For example, Jeni's Splendid Ice Creams has launched a twist on a Pop-Tarts favorite.

It's a frozen version of the popular classic brown sugar cinnamon flavor.

Customers can buy a new ice cream treat made by Häagen-Dazs in grocery stores.

Known as the Cultured Crème, it's actually a combination of ice cream and yogurt.

It can be purchased in the following flavors:

  • Vanilla Bean
  • Strawberry
  • Coffee
  • Lemon
  • Blueberry
  • Black Cherry

Target is selling four-ounce containers of the flavors for only $1.79.

And McDonald's recently brought back a few freeze treats, which are as follows:

  • Frozen Coca-Cola
  • Frozen Fanta Blue Raspberry
  • Frozen Sprite Lymonade
