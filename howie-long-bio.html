Howie Long: Professional career

As an all-around athlete, Howie was a high school basketball and track star and a boxing champion for the Northern Collegiate.

He started his professional career by playing NFL as he was selected in the second round of the 1981 NFL Draft by the Oakland Raiders.

Howie, wearing jersey number 75, played 13 seasons for the Oakland Raiders in the defensive end position and appeared eight times in Pro Bowl in his professional career.

His professional career as an NFL player was high in 1983 with 13 sacks and a career-high five on October 2, 1983, against the Washington Redskins.

The Sommerville native had high expectations in his early career as in 1986; he had told football Digest that he desired financial security and wished to be in the Hall of Fame, which was his goal for life.

The Bay stater was also made into the first All-Pro team consecutively from 1983 to 1985.

He was also twice named to the second All-Pro team in 1986 and 1989. Besides that, Howie Long also earned the NFL Alumni Defensive Lineman of the Year in 1985
