Huge radio host set to step down from breakfast show to focus on TV career

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, August 16, 2024

ROMAN KEMP is finally going to get a lie-in as he is poised to announce his departure from Capital FM’s breakfast show, I can reveal.

He is stepping down after seven years to focus on TV, having got a presenting job on BBC’s The One Show in the summer.

The news will be a shock to fans who have enjoyed waking up to his upbeat banter with pals Chris Stark and Sian Welby.

He has been a big hit on the station, especially as he is mates with so many pop stars, including Lewis Capaldi and Ed Sheeran.

A telly source said: “Roman has loved his time at Capital FM and it has been a huge learning curve for him over the years.

“But he feels now is the right time to pass the baton on to someone else. He’s got several big TV opportunities on the table and thinks the natural next step is to focus on those.”

As well as starring on I’m A Celeb in 2019, the son of Spandau Ballet's Martin has also made an award-nominated BBC documentary about mental health.

Single Roman, has previously opened up about the challenges of dating alongside his breakfast gig.

He said: “If you want to point me in the direction of a girl that wants to wake up at 4.50am and wants to go to bed at 9pm, please do.”

DRAKE’S PARTIES POOPER

THE PET SHOP BOYS have hit out at Drake for apparently using their lyrics without asking them.

The synth-pop duo claim the Canadian star took parts of 1986 No1 West End Girls on his All The Parties record with no credit or permission.

Taking to X, the pair wrote: “Surprising to hear Drake singing the chorus of West End Girls in the track All The Parties on his new album.

“No credit given or permission requested.”

They also included the hashtag #kobalt music, the management and publishing firm they signed to in 2013.

In the track from his new album For All The Dogs, Drake sings: “And it’s six, our town a dead-end world.

“East End boys and West End girls, yeah.

“East End boys and West End girls.”

It comes after multi-Grammy winner Drake, shocked fans on Friday by announcing he was taking a break from music after suffering from stomach issues.

He said: “I need to focus on my health, first and foremost – and I’ll talk about that soon enough.

"Nothing crazy, but you know, I want people to be healthy in life, and I’ve been having the craziest problems for years with my stomach.”

JUNIOR: I NEVER SWEAR

UP-and-coming singer Junior Andre seems nothing like his party-mad mum Katie Price.

The son of the ex-glamour model and Peter Andre avoids swearing in his songs – and would much prefer to have a bar of chocolate instead of shots in his dressing room.

Junior’s single Only One dropped last month and he said of his lyrics: “I don’t swear on my music, I never swear.

“There are so many things to write about, you don’t need to swear.

“Families can listen to my music.”

On the 3ShotsOfTequila podcast, he added: “I don’t even drink, to be totally honest.

“I don’t really like the taste of most alcohol. On my rider I would have chocolate, Lucozade and water.”

But Junior has admitted he does take after Dad when it comes to his physique.

Asked about Peter’s abs in the 1995 Mysterious Girl video, he said: “I have got his genetics as well.”
