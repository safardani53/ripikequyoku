Jonas Brothers The Tour: How can I buy tickets?

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, June 30, 2024

THE JONAS Brothers are set to embark on a 90-date world tour dubbed The Tour in August.

Here's what you need to know about the band's biggest tour to date and how you can score tickets.

How can I get tickets?

Tickets to Jonas Brothers' The Tour will be up for sale on Ticketmaster.

Fans wishing to catch the famous siblings' North American shows may register for Live Nation's Verified Fan presale from July 27 to July 31, 10pm ET.

Lucky fans who are selected can purchase tickets during the presale on August 3.

Ticket presale for Europe dates starts on August 2 and ends with a general sale on August 4.

The general sale for US dates also begins on August 4 at 10am ET at jonasbrothers.com.

In April 2023, the band told SiriusXM's Radio Andy that they are planning to perform “pretty much every song from all the albums.”

"Correct, a 17-hour show," Joe Jonas quipped.

Nick Jonas explained that their five-night Broadway residency in March 2023 inspired their setlist for The Tour.

“For those that don’t know, we basically played five nights and picked a different album that we spotlit for the first act of the show,” he said.

“We played it in its entirety. Which, for a lot of these songs, we’ve never even played them, or we hadn’t played them in 15-plus years.”

Are they releasing new music?

On May 12, 2023, Jonas Brothers released their sixth studio album, The Album, which featured 12 new songs, including a collaboration with Jon Bellion.

In an interview with Today, Nick said that the record reflects their journey as artists: "I think this one does a good job of embodying kind of where we are as fathers, where we are as husbands and just brothers.

"We set out to just say, if you're gonna listen to any one of our albums we think this is the album you should listen to.

"This is the one that is the most quintessential Jonas Brothers."

Speaking with Variety, Kevin Jonas also dished that they took inspiration from 70s bands like Bee Gees.

"They were a huge influence in our lives, growing up and listening to them with our father,” he said.
